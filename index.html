Latin Usage


Albeit there is authority to reduce legalese in contract1 and recommendations to draft in plain language,2 Latin terminology in law continues to hold enduring relevance, as it encapsulates centuries of jurisprudential wisdom in concise and universally recognised expressions.

Whilst pleadings should be clear and concise,3 courts worldwide continue to invoke expressions like Ad idem and Mens rea, underscoring their interpretive clarity and authoritative weight. Latin terms and phrases are still abundantly used in personal injury claims, whereas Magistrates' replies and judgments rely similarly for shorthand.

1 Beadica 231 CC v Trustees for the Time Being of the Oregon Trust 2020 (5) SA 247 (CC) at para [97].

2 See Uniform Rule 20, and Magistrates' Court Rule 15, respectively.

3 Marnewick C. Litigation Skills for South African Lawyers (3rd Ed, LexisNexis South Africa: 2012) at 422.
